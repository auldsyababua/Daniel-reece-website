      We’ll guide you every step of the way

      Through a structured curriculum with recorded sessions, we’ll guide you in creating a safe space to reflect, dream big, and plan the next season of your career and life—at your own pace.

      There are no prerequisites or requirements to join The Annual Review. Whether you're a seasoned planner or new to the concept, you're welcome here.

      1. Prep

      We'll kick off by helping you gather the tools and materials you'll need for a successful review. You’ll set your intentions and prepare mentally for the reflection process ahead.

      2. Past

      Reflect on the past year by revisiting key memories, accomplishments, and lessons learned. We’ll help you identify meaningful moments and create a gratitude list to close the year with a sense of completion.

      3. Present 

      In this session, we’ll focus on connecting the dots between all the artifacts you’ve collected so far. You’ll identify what ideas or new directions are most alive and exciting for you right now, tapping into the power of your emotions as potent sources of information.

      4. Future

      Using everything you’ve learned and gathered up until now, this is the time to look ahead to the future and craft a clear vision for the next season of your life. You’ll set goals and intentions, and choose a set of projects that concretely turn your priorities into reality.

      5. Follow-through

      We’ll show you how to set up systems and routines to create a scaffolding for your new direction. Learn strategies to revisit your goals regularly and, even more importantly, tweak and tune them as you discover more about what is working and what you truly want.

      Are there any requirements to participate in the program?

      The beauty of The Annual Review is its accessibility – there are no specific prerequisites or requirements to join. Whether you're a seasoned planner or new to the concept, you're welcome here.
      ‍
      That said, to get the most out of the experience, it can be incredibly helpful to have some raw materials from the past year at your fingertips. Things like journals, personal notes, photographs, or any other records that capture your experiences and thoughts from the year will be valuable. They'll help you start with something concrete and make your process more personal and meaningful.

      Don't worry if you don't have an extensive collection of such materials. Even a few notes or memorable photos can serve as a great starting point for reflection and planning. The program is designed to be flexible and accommodating to your unique journey, and we’ll guide you in how and where to collect any existing material you may have.

      So, come as you are, with whatever you have from the past year, and we'll take it from there!
