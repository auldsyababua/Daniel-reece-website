You’re the kind of person who knows things could work so much better

You see it every day—the bottlenecks, the miscommunications, the search for information you’re sure is buried somewhere. 

And you’ve felt it, too, that frustration of knowing how much smoother things could be if just a few key practices were adopted across the board.

So you’ve stepped up—whether it’s teaching others, advocating for better ways of working, or leading by example. 

But let’s be honest: it can feel like an uphill battle, especially when the changes you’re making don’t always catch on with the people around you.

Now, you’re not just looking to get by – you want more! 

You’re ready to move from tinkering with ideas to creating something meaningful—work that flows, systems that make sense, and tools that work for you, not against you. And even though you know it’s possible, the path to getting there feels overwhelming.

The good news? You don’t have to figure it all out on your own. We’re here to help you make that leap, step by step.

Introducing the new Second Brain Membership 

We bring together knowledge leaders like you so that you can create workspaces where information is easy to find, communication is smooth, and you (and your team) are making a bigger impact than you ever thought possible.

When you build a Second Brain, you’re not just managing information—you’re unlocking your potential. And when you apply those principles to your team or clients, you’re fostering what we call team intelligence: a collective ability to tackle challenges, make smarter decisions, and create meaningful impact.

How you benefit…

  • Combat information overwhelm and free up your attention for the most creative and interesting endeavors
  • Create a digital environment that promotes flow, clarity, and peace of mind so you can do your best work at any time
  • Have a playground of your thoughts, ideas, resources, and connections to bring to life the potential that has been locked up inside you 
  • Know exactly where to start when you sit down to work, so you can achieve lift-off on long-awaited projects and take them to the finish line
  • Focus your time and energy on the priorities that are most important to you
  • Become known as the person who gets stuff done and others look to for advice 

How your team, organization, or clients benefit…

  • Communication and teamwork flow easily, so projects move forward faster than ever while everyone stays on the same page
  • Information is organized and easy to find, with less digital clutter and everything important in one place so no time is wasted searching
  • Everyone stays focused on what really matters, putting energy into high-impact projects instead of getting distracted
  • Knowledge spreads within your organization so that new people get up to speed quickly and delegation becomes easy
  • Decisions are made faster and smarter, with team members able to think critically and act quickly
  • The team is always improving, with a culture that encourages learning, experimenting, and iterating

Monthly Themes

How we help you achieve your full potential in the Second Brain Membership

Each month, we’ll focus on a key theme, creating a structured path for the year and giving you the tools and strategies to thrive both individually and collaboratively.

April – Streamlining Communication (Emails & IMs)

You’re wasting precious minutes hunting for the email you need to move forward on a project, or worse, dealing with repeated requests to resend information you’ve already shared. Whether you’re collaborating with a team or managing client communication, inefficiencies like these can create unnecessary friction and waste valuable time. This month, we’ll establish clear guidelines for emails, instant messages, and team communication so that every interaction serves a purpose. By focusing on clarity, boundaries, and actionable requests, you’ll create workflows that foster collaboration and reduce friction.

May – Getting Things Done

You’re ending your days unsure of how much you actually accomplished, feeling like you’re being pulled in too many directions at once. Maybe you’re stuck waiting on others to move projects forward, or you’re overwhelmed by an unending to-do list. In May, we’ll build systems for task management, calendar planning, and weekly reviews to help you minimize procrastination and maximize focus. By the end of the month, you’ll have a reliable process for prioritizing your work and making consistent progress toward your goals.

June – Starting & Following Through on Projects

Projects are the most important unit of progress in the modern work environment—they’re how you move things forward and get meaningful results. But managing projects can feel overwhelming, especially when you’re juggling multiple priorities or struggling to decide what to tackle first. This month, we’ll focus on the essentials of project management, including how to prioritize effectively, break down large projects into manageable steps, and follow through to completion. By the end of June, you’ll have a framework to confidently manage multiple projects at once, reduce overwhelm, and consistently deliver results.

July – Resetting Your System at Mid-Year

As the year progresses, your priorities and tools can drift out of alignment. July is the perfect time to step back, reflect on your progress, and recalibrate your systems for the months ahead. Together, we’ll refresh your PARA structure, adjust your toolkit, and refine your workflows so that you’re ready to finish the year strong.

August – Leveraging AI

You’ve seen others using AI to get more done with less effort, and you’re wondering, why not me? AI is quickly becoming an anchor for many systems and tools, making it essential to understand how to use it effectively. That’s why this month is dedicated to exploring how AI can streamline your workflows, automate repetitive tasks, and enhance decision-making. By the end of August, you’ll know how to select the right AI tools and use them to supercharge your productivity in the most impactful ways.

September – Running Effective Meetings and Calls

We’ve all been there: a meeting that should have been an email or a long email chain that really needed to be a meeting. Poorly planned communication like this wastes time and leaves everyone feeling frustrated. This month, we’ll tackle how to design and run meetings that drive action, clarify objectives, and foster collaboration—whether they’re with your team or external stakeholders. By the end of September, you’ll have a system to ensure that every meeting and call is purposeful, productive, and worth everyone’s time.

October – Building Your Internal Knowledge Base

Delegating tasks or onboarding new team members can feel overwhelming when the necessary resources and documentation aren’t in place. Too often, standard operating procedures (SOPs) are created but rarely revisited, becoming just another forgotten document. This month, we’ll focus on creating low-friction, actionable SOPs that people actually use and interact with regularly. You’ll also learn the best tools and techniques to build a knowledge base that simplifies delegation and makes onboarding new team members seamless. By the end of October, you’ll have an efficient system that captures your and your team’s intelligence.

November – Optimizing Your Tools (Revisit)

As your goals evolve throughout the year, your tools may need fine-tuning to keep up. In November, we’ll take stock of your toolkit and apply lessons learned to refine and optimize your setup. By the end of the month, you’ll feel confident that your tools are working in harmony with your priorities and supporting you for what’s ahead.

December – Reviewing Your Year & Planning Ahead

As the year ends, it’s time to take stock of everything you’ve accomplished—not just to prepare for official performance evaluations, but to truly appreciate your own progress. It’s also an opportunity to reflect on the challenges you faced and the lessons you learned, so you can avoid repeating the same frustrations in the future. By capturing these insights, you’ll close the feedback loop, turn setbacks into learning opportunities, and head into the new year with clear goals and a stronger system to support them.

January – Clearing Your Workspace

A cluttered workspace, whether physical or digital, creates overwhelm and makes it harder to focus. In January, we’ll work together to declutter your personal and shared environment and archive anything that’s no longer serving you. By the end of the month, you’ll have a clean, inviting workspace that motivates you to tackle your most important projects with clarity and energy.

February – Organizing Information with PARA

Disorganized information can leave you feeling overwhelmed and unsure where to start. This month, we’ll dive into the PARA Method (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives) to create a structure that’s tailored to your needs. By applying PARA across your personal and shared spaces, you’ll not only bring order to your information but also ensure that everything you need is exactly where you can find it.

March – Choosing Your Tools

Choosing the right tools can be challenging, especially if you’re working within an existing team or organization where you’re not the decision-maker. This month, we’ll help you take stock of your current toolkit, evaluate what’s working, and find ways to customize and adapt to your specific needs—even within constraints. By the end of March, you’ll feel confident that you can make the most of your tools, whether you’re designing your own system or maximizing what’s already in place.

Throughout the Year, We’ll Discuss…

  • How to get others on board when introducing new practices
  • How to teach and explain Second Brain concepts effectively
  • How to seamlessly leverage AI in your daily workflows.
  • How to align decisions with your values, principles, and core motivations
  • How to explore personal growth and self-understanding as keys to fulfillment

*The monthly themes may be subject to change. We will listen to members’ feedback and make adjustments on the fly.

Answers to your frequently asked questions

What kind of events do you offer as part of the Membership?

Tiago hosts monthly Q&A sessions where you can get direct coaching from him (this is currently the only way to get direct access to him). He will also lead regular specialized workshops where he shares his most current experiments and discoveries in areas such as AI, digital productivity, and knowledge management.

‍

In addition, we offer:

  • Weekly Office Hours to teach, discuss, and answer questions around the monthly theme
  • Weekly Review sessions to help you clear your digital environment and plan for the upcoming week
  • Expert workshops to help you level up your knowledge management practice
Who is the Membership not for?

The Second Brain Membership is not a great fit for those who are completely new to the concept of building a Second Brain. This isn’t a beginner’s course—we’re focused on refining and extending your existing system, especially in ways that help you collaborate more effectively at work or in your business. 

‍

(If you’re new here, check out our book or self-paced course.)

‍

It’s also not ideal for anyone looking to be told exactly what to do. The membership thrives on active participation and collaboration, where members share insights, contribute ideas, and learn from each other. The more you engage, the more you’ll get out of it. Together, we create a shared knowledge base that reflects the very principles of the Second Brain—fostering continuous learning and growth for everyone.

Where do you host the Second Brain Membership?

We’re hosting the Second Brain Membership on the popular community platform Circle. Circle is also available as a mobile app for Apple and Android so you can access the membership conveniently on the go. Once you sign up, you’ll receive your login.

What's the difference between the quarterly and annual Membership?

The quarterly Membership renews every 3 months (90 days) and the annual Membership every 365 days.

‍

Both the quarterly and annual Membership will automatically renew until you cancel them. You'll receive a reminder email 7 days before your renewal date.

Do you offer discounts for the Second Brain Membership?

We're dedicated to making the Second Brain Membership accessible to a diverse range of people. If you're affiliated with a non-profit organization, NGO, educational institution, or are a faculty member, please reach out to support@fortelabs.com to inquire about a discount.

‍

We also support Purchasing Power Parity Pricing. Based on your location, you might qualify for a reduced ticket price for the Second Brain Membership. If you do, you’ll automatically see a banner with a discount code. Make sure to disable any VPN since this will block the banner from showing.

What's the refund policy for the Second Brain Membership?

We do not offer refunds for the Second Brain Membership.
