What people ask us about Building a Second Brain

These are the questions that might pop into your head right now.

Is this just another productivity system I’ll stop using eventually?

You're probably wary of yet another system promising the moon with this or that new app. There are far too many feel-good, wishy-washy systems cooked up by people looking to make a quick buck.

Here’s what makes Building a Second Brain different: It’s built on timeless and unchanging principles.

You see, while technology and trends are constantly changing, the heart of the creative process remains fundamentally the same:
‍
• Capturing information from the outside world
• Organizing it in a way that lets you easily find and use it
• Distilling it down to the best ideas
• And then expressing your ideas in your unique voice through writing, speaking, designing, or teaching

This is the C.O.D.E System, our 4-step process for consistently turning the information you consume into creative output and concrete results. And it works in any app or platform you prefer to use.

Will this work in [insert your favorite notetaking app]?

Building a Second Brain is tool-agnostic, meaning our system will work in any software you prefer. Our students use Evernote, Notion, Microsoft OneNote, Apple Notes, Roam Research, Obsidian, DevonThink, Bear, Google Keep, and many other tools.

Will I spend more time glued to my devices organizing information?

Organizing for the sake of organizing information isn’t the goal of Building a Second Brain. The goal is to build a system you can rely on to consistently get more and better work done, in less time, with less stress. That way, you’ll feel accomplished when you’re working and relaxed when you’re not.

You can spend more time with the people you love and be truly present, confident that you’re not losing something, dropping balls, or falling behind.

Is this just another self-improvement fad?

Building a Second Brain is actually the opposite of self-improvement. It is about optimizing a system outside of yourself. A system not subject to your limitations and constraints. It is designed to leave you happily unoptimized, free to roam, to wonder, to wander toward whatever makes you feel alive here and now in each moment.

Remember everything. Achieve anything.

When you leverage technology to master the flow of information through your life, you can accomplish anything you set your mind to.

But if you continue to rely on your fragile biological brain, you’ll likely become ever more overwhelmed by the explosive growth in the complexity of your life.
